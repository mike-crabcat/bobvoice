# Lesson 9: Useful Phrases and Getting Help

## Learning Objectives
- Say you don't understand and ask people to repeat
- Ask for help in simple situations
- Handle basic emergencies
- Express simple needs and feelings

## Steps
- [ ] Step 1: Learn comprehension phrases: "Je ne comprends pas" (I don't understand), "Pouvez-vous répéter?" (Can you repeat?), "Plus lentement, s'il vous plaît" (More slowly, please)
- [ ] Step 2: Learn to express needs: "J'ai besoin de..." (I need...), "J'ai faim" (I'm hungry), "J'ai soif" (I'm thirsty), "Je suis fatigué" (I'm tired), "Je suis perdu" (I'm lost)
- [ ] Step 3: Learn emergency phrases: "Au secours!" (Help!), "Appelez un médecin" (Call a doctor), "C'est urgent", "Je ne me sens pas bien" (I don't feel well)
- [ ] Step 4: Learn problem-solving: "Il y a un problème" (There's a problem), "Ce n'est pas ce que j'ai commandé" (This isn't what I ordered), "Je suis allergique à..." (I'm allergic to...)
- [ ] Step 5: Learn conversation fillers: "D'accord" (OK), "C'est vrai" (That's true), "Bien sûr" (Of course), "Enchanté" (Nice to meet you)
- [ ] Step 6: Role-play — at a restaurant your order is wrong, explain the problem politely, then tell the waiter you're allergic to something, ask them to repeat the specials slowly

## Review from previous lessons
