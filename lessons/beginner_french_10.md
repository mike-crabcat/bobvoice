# Lesson 10: Your Vacation Day in France

## Learning Objectives
- Combine all learned vocabulary into fluid conversation
- Navigate a full day as a tourist in France
- Handle real-world situations from morning to evening
- Demonstrate confidence with beginner French

## Steps
- [ ] Step 1: Morning — at the hotel, ask about breakfast hours, get the wifi password, check what time checkout is
- [ ] Step 2: Mid-morning — at the bakery, buy a baguette and some pastries, ask the price, pay and thank the baker
- [ ] Step 3: Afternoon — visit an attraction, buy a ticket, ask about opening hours and where the entrance is, ask if there's a queue
- [ ] Step 4: Late afternoon — stop at a café, order a drink, say whether you like it, ask for directions to somewhere nearby
- [ ] Step 5: Evening — at a restaurant, get a table, order a meal, say what you like and don't like, ask for the bill
- [ ] Step 6: End of day — say goodbye to the hotel receptionist, mention you had a great time, check out for tomorrow

## Review from previous lessons
This lesson uses everything from the entire course:
- Lesson 1: Greetings, please, thank you, excuse me
- Lesson 2: Ordering at a café, asking for the bill
- Lesson 3: Restaurant phrases, ordering food, asking about the menu
- Lesson 4: Directions, transport, where is...?
- Lesson 5: Hotel check-in, facilities, problems
- Lesson 6: Shopping, prices, quantities
- Lesson 7: Days, times, tickets, queues
- Lesson 8: Likes, dislikes, making choices
- Lesson 9: Asking for help, saying you don't understand, emergencies

## Final Quiz
- [ ] Q1: Walk into a bakery, greet the baker, order two croissants and a baguette, and ask how much it costs
- [ ] Q2: At a restaurant, ask for a table, say you're vegetarian, order a main course, and ask for the bill
- [ ] Q3: You're lost near the hotel. Ask someone for directions, say you don't understand, ask them to repeat slowly
- [ ] Q4: Free conversation — the teacher will throw surprise vacation situations at you using vocabulary from all previous lessons
